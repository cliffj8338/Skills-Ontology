The Plan, Honestly

Pottstown to the Black Hills is roughly 1,700 miles. Round-trip, that’s about five travel days no matter how you slice it.

So this trip is a clockwise loop — out via I-80 to South Dakota, north into the prairies, west across Big Sky country, down through Yellowstone (which spans MT/WY/ID), and back through Wyoming and the long road home. Long driving days at the bookends, slower days in the middle where the good stuff lives.

Two boys. Five new state pins. One big circle. A reasonable hotel budget.

Logistics & Pro-Tips

Book These Now

  • West Yellowstone lodging (Kelly Inn or similar) — June fills 6+ months ahead
  • Medora, ND (Badlands Motel or Rough Riders) — tiny town, fills fast
  • Pitchfork Fondue + Medora Musical bundle ($89.95/adult, kids 12 & under eat free)
  • America the Beautiful Pass ($80 — pays for itself by Yellowstone)
  • Cody Nite Rodeo tickets

Pack for Real

  • Layers — Yellowstone can be 35°F at sunrise, 80°F by 2pm
  • Headlamps for everyone (Craters of the Moon caves)
  • Binoculars, even cheap ones, for wildlife
  • A real cooler — in-park food is awful and overpriced
  • Bear spray for Yellowstone hikes (rent in West Yellowstone, ~$10/day)
  • Junior Ranger habit: ask at every visitor center

Driving Realities

  • Cell service vanishes in Yellowstone, TRNP, and big stretches of MT/WY — download offline maps
  • Gas up at every half-tank in WY/MT/SD; some stretches go 80+ mi between stations
  • Mountain gas runs ~$4+/gal vs $3.20-3.50 in the Midwest
  • Watch for wildlife at dawn and dusk, especially in Yellowstone & TRNP

Where to Save / Splurge

  • Save: Pack lunches from grocery stores — saves ~$200 over the trip
  • Save: Hotel breakfast at Hampton/Holiday Inn = no breakfast budget
  • Splurge: Pitchfork + Musical (Day 7) — once-in-a-lifetime
  • Splurge: Cody Nite Rodeo (Day 13) — the boys will remember it forever
  • Skip: Helicopter tours over Yellowstone ($400+/family) — the boardwalks are better
