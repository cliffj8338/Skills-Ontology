The Plan, Honestly

Pottstown to the Black Hills is roughly 1,700 miles. Round-trip, that’s about five travel days no matter how you slice it.

So this trip is a clockwise loop — out via I-80 to South Dakota, north into the prairies, west across Big Sky country, down through Yellowstone (which spans MT/WY/ID), and back through Wyoming and the long road home. Long driving days at the bookends, slower days in the middle where the good stuff lives.

Two boys. Eight new state pins. One big circle. A reasonable hotel budget.

Yellowstone Survival Guide

The next two days are the hardest logistical days of the trip. Read this in the parking lot before Day 10 starts.

Gate Hours

West Entrance is open 24/7 in summer, but the visitor center opens at 8am. Be on the road by 6:30 to beat the 9am rush at Old Faithful.

Cell Service

Effectively zero cellular in most of the park, but Starlink in the truck handles it. Park anywhere with sky overhead and you've got wifi for nav, calls, photos to family. Solid native coverage at Old Faithful, Canyon Village, and Mammoth if you're walking and the truck's parked far away.

Fuel Inside the Park

Stations at Old Faithful, Canyon, Fishing Bridge, Tower, Mammoth. ~30% pricier than West Yellowstone — fill up there first.

Food & Groceries

In-park dining is mediocre and overpriced. Stock the cooler at the Market Place in West Yellowstone — and the cabin kitchenette means real lunches packed before each park day. Save $40-60/day vs in-park dining.

Bear Safety

Rent bear spray in West Yellowstone (~$10/day vs $50 to buy). Required for any off-boardwalk hiking. Stay 100 yards from bears, 25 from bison.

If a Road Closes

Sections close suddenly — wildlife jams, construction, weather. Check nps.gov/yell at breakfast. The Lower Loop has multiple workarounds; the East Entrance (Sylvan Pass) is the most fragile.

The Bison Jam Reality

You will sit in traffic behind a bison. Possibly for an hour. This is the price. Don't honk. Don't get out. Take pictures.

Day 13 Re-Entry

You go BACK through Yellowstone east-to-west on Day 13 via Sylvan Pass to reach Cody. If Sylvan is closed (snow even in June possible), reroute via Beartooth Hwy through Red Lodge — adds 2 hours but is genuinely the most beautiful drive in America.

The Stargazing Plan

Telescope nights, ranked. The first week of the trip is moonless — rare and valuable. Use it.

DAY 4 · TUE 6/16 · WALL, SD
Badlands Country
🌑 1% waxing crescent · sets 21:08 · effectively MOONLESS
Badlands NP earned official Dark Sky designation in 2025. Walk 5 minutes from the Best Western parking lot for a true horizon-to-horizon view.EyesMilky Way core rising in the SE around 22:30. Summer Triangle (Vega, Deneb, Altair) overhead.TelescopeM13 globular cluster in Hercules · Ring Nebula M57 in Lyra · Albireo (the gold/blue double star).
★ DAY 5 · WED 6/17 · KEYSTONE, SD
The Marquee Night
🌒 4% waxing crescent · sets 21:32
Rare west-sky planet alignment in evening twilight: Mercury + Venus + Jupiter + thin crescent moon all within a few degrees. Look WNW about 30 min after sunset (~21:00 MT).TelescopeJupiter's 4 Galilean moons. Venus showing a phase. Mercury (rare to catch). Then once full dark hits ~22:00: M51 Whirlpool Galaxy in Ursa Major, M81/M82 galaxy pair.BonusBoys can spot Polaris and find their way home from anywhere on Earth — start the lesson here.
DAYS 6-7 · THU-FRI 6/18-19 · MEDORA, ND
Theodore Roosevelt Country
🌒 14-22% crescent · sets ~23:30
TRNP is also a designated Dark Sky park. Walk to the bluff above ElkHorn Quarters or drive 5 min into TRNP after dinner.EyesScorpius low in the south — one of the few constellations that actually looks like its name.TelescopeSaturn rises after midnight (Day 7) — rings visible. DAYS 9-10 · SUN-MON 6/21-22 · YELLOWSTONE

Logistics & Pro-Tips

Book These Now

  • West Yellowstone lodging — June fills 6+ months ahead. Book the day you commit to the trip.
  • Medora, ND (ElkHorn Quarters or Rough Riders) — tiny town, fills fast
  • Pitchfork Fondue + Medora Musical bundle ($89.95/adult, kids 12 & under eat free)
  • America the Beautiful Pass ($80 — pays for itself by Yellowstone)
  • Cody Nite Rodeo tickets

Pack for Real

  • Layers — Yellowstone can be 35°F at sunrise, 80°F by 2pm
  • Headlamps for everyone (Craters of the Moon caves)
  • Binoculars, even cheap ones, for wildlife
  • A real cooler — in-park food is awful and overpriced
  • Bear spray for Yellowstone hikes (rent in West Yellowstone, ~$10/day)
  • Junior Ranger habit: ask at every visitor center

Driving Realities

  • Cell service vanishes in Yellowstone, TRNP, and big stretches of MT/WY — Starlink in the truck handles it. Still download offline Google Maps as backup.
  • Gas up at every half-tank in WY/MT/SD; some stretches go 80+ mi between stations
  • Mountain gas runs ~$4+/gal vs $3.20-3.50 in the Midwest
  • Watch for wildlife at dawn and dusk, especially in Yellowstone & TRNP

Where to Save / Splurge

  • Save: Pack lunches from grocery stores — saves ~$200 over the trip
  • Save: Hotel breakfast at Hampton/Holiday Inn = no breakfast budget
  • Splurge: Pitchfork + Musical (Day 7) — once-in-a-lifetime
  • Splurge: Cody Nite Rodeo (Day 13) — the boys will remember it forever
  • Skip: Helicopter tours over Yellowstone ($400+/family) — the boardwalks are better
